About

The IEEE Vehicular Technology Society (VTS) Japan Chapter, Seoul Chapter, Singapore Chapter, and Taipei Chapter are cooperatively hosting Asia Pacific Wireless Communications Symposium (APWCS) every year.
This symposium aims at providing the platform for researchers from the Asia Pacific area to share fresh results, call for comments or collaborations and exchange innovative ideas of the leading edge research in wireless technologies.

Welcome Message

Welcome Message from the General Chairs

On behalf of the Organizing Committee, it is our great pleasure to welcome you to the 15th IEEE Vehicular Technology Society Asia Pacific Wireless Communications Symposium (IEEE VTS APWCS 2018) to be held on August 22th to 24th, 2018, at National Chiao Tung University, Hsinchu, the Silicon Valley of Taiwan.

APWCS 2018 is technically sponsored by the IEEE VTS, and jointly sponsored by the IEEE VTS Japan Chapter, Seoul Chapter, Singapore Chapter, and Taipei Chapter. APWCS has pursued promoting information exchange and improving collaboration among researchers, scholars, engineers, and leaders working in the wireless communications field, especially, across Asia and Pacific Regions.

This year, with the greatest efforts from our Technical Program Committee (TPC), we celebrate the 15th year of APWCS with excellent technical programs on wireless communications and networking. The keynote and invited speeches will reveal the emerging technologies and future research trends; while the technical sessions demonstrate the state-of-art research by distinguished researchers. We are grateful for the efforts from all our TPC members.

Also, we would like to show our appreciation to the Organizing Committee Members, the keynote and invited speakers, the paper reviewers, the APWCS Board of Governors, and, last but not least, the authors for their great contribution and support. We hope you will enjoy the technical programs and social activities in APWCS 2018. We believe you will have a fantastic experience enjoying the mixed technological and cultural varieties of Hsinchu, Taiwan.

Information for Authors

Guidelines for Presentations

Each presenter has 20 minutes: 17 minutes for presentation and 3 minutes for Q&A. Session chairs will be very strict about timing and will stop you when your time is up even if you have not finished. Strict adherence to timing within sessions is important in order to allow attendees to move between sessions.

Furthermore, every presenter is required to show up 10 minutes early before the session begins. Upon your arrival to the session, please submit your brief biography to the session chair and upload your presentation material on the laptop in the session room. It is recommended to prepare the presentation material in both powerpoint and pdf formats.
